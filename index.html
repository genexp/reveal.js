I've been lucky to work with a lot of software teams

  • I've been a part of a dozen or so different software teams across various or so different companies
  • For almost all of these teams, I've been in a leadership role. In fact, my first job out of college, and almost every job since, has been as either team or department lead.
  • over the years, my career arc has gone in the opposite direction of most of the other software leaders I've met.

A lot of teams are good, some are great

  • I was shocked to find that a lot of managers have no idea what they were doing.
  • Congrats, almost everyone is now Agile. What does that mean?
  • Also consult with companies that want more from their dev teams

If you're not technical, don't fake it

  • There are few phrases that anger software engineers more than, “I used to code”
  • If I were to ask LeBron James how much time he needs to prepare for a game, I’m sure he’d be amused if I disagreed because I played basketball in high school. Software engineers get the equivalent all the time.

Take bullets

Ask questions

Be available for answers.

Measure success two fold: Are we happy? Are we productive?
